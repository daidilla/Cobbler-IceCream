Items/Ingredients Needed

Here is a list of ingredients needed to complete this recipe

  • 8 fresh peaches The peaches being fresh is optional but in my opinion the recipe is the best with fresh peaches. You are welcome to use canned peaches or frozen peaches. If using frozen or canned peaches you would need approximately 4 cups of peaches. If using fresh peaches please peel, pit, and slice into wedges.

  • 1/2 cup of white sugar
  • 1/2 cup of brown sugar
  • 1/4th teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/8th teaspoon of ground nutmeg
  • 1 teaspoon of fresh lemon juice or bottled

    • Again, fresh ingredients are optional but are the preference for the best turnout of this simple recipe.

  • 2 teaspoons of cornstarch
  • 1 cup of all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon of baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon of salt
  • 6 tablespoons of unsalted butter

    • The butter for this recipe needs to be chilled and cut into thin, small pieces

  • 1/4th cup of boiling water

Directions of Assembly

Here are the instructions to combining the above listed ingredients and baking the best peach cobbler.

  1. Preheat your oven to approximately 425 degrees F (220 degrees C)
  2. In a large bowl, combine your sliced peaches, 1/4 cup of white sugar, 1/4 cup of your brown sugar, your 1/4th teaspoon of cinnamon, your nutmeg, lemon juice, and cornstarch.

    3. Toss the peaches in the bowl to coat evenly. Make sure all peaches are cover to the best of your ability. Afterwards, pour the peaches into a baking dish and place in the preheated oven for 10-12 minutes.

  3. As the peaches bake, in another large bowl, combine your measured flour, the rest of your white and brown sugar, baking powder, and salt.After combining, Blend in your butter with your fingertips until mixture is coarse. Finially, stir in your hot water until just combined. **This is your crust or topping to your cobbler**

  4. Your peaches shoukd be done and you can remove them from the oven. After removal, drop spponfuls of your crust mixture onto the peaches. Before placing the cobbler back into the oven, mix small amounts of brown and white sugar together and spinkle on top of the entire cobbler.

  5. Place the cobbler dish back into the oven for approximately 30 minutes or until the crust is brown to your liking.
  6. Once topping is brown to your liking, remove cobbler from oven and let rest for 10-14 minutes and then serve with whipping cream or ice cream (optional)
